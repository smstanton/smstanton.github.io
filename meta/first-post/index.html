Hello

Hello, I’m Stephen. Welcome to NoSlackDev.

This first post is also represented in the About section.

The idea behind this blog is still forming, but instead of trying to have a fully fleshed out idea before starting it, I thought it wise to just start… and see where it goes.

This is a blog about learning and improving in general, with a particular focus around software development. I seek to guide myself through a growing curriculum of classes, books, projects, and other activities to improve myself in many ways: as a technologist, a developer, a problem solver, a thinker, a builder, a consultant, and in a variety of other skills and interests that I may write about here. Learning should never end, so this journey will be one that doesn’t end - but it should have many points of interest along the way, and you are welcome to join me in visiting them if you are on a similar journey.

I start out as a technology professional with a passing understanding of many technologies. I’ve tinkered with technology from the time I was young, took a lot of classes, read a lot of books, earned a few certifications, and contributed to many projects. I know enough to get by, pretty well in some cases. Yet I, like many, aspire to improve.

Curriculum

I have a curriculum that I will follow, which was constructed using the OSSU Computer Science education as a starting point. I’ve added many other objectives to the curriculum according to my interests and personal learning style, and I will continually refine this curriculum over time. At some point, I may publish the entire backlog of curriculum if there is interest. For now, I will publish those things that I am actively working on, along with a preview of what I think is coming up next.

I am a completist by nature. When I learn something, I have to go deep. I can barely watch a movie without digging into all the facts I can find about it. I’m bringing this mentality to my approach here, and am starting at the beginning on many of the topics I pursue. I will be relearning many things that I’ve learned in the past, but I’m taking a more thorough approach this time through, and publishing my writing, source, and useful notes as I go.

How to follow along

The curriculum is divided into objectives, which are individual efforts such as finishing a course, reading a book, finishing a project, or earning a certificate. Objectives don’t have a defined timeline: some will be short-term, others long. Each objective will have its own category.

Objectives where there is a development component will have a corresponding repository on my GitHub site, where I will keep my source updated as I go.

There will be some categories that don’t map directly to an objective. At some point down the road, I will organize the Categories page to make this distinction more apparent.

Posts will be tagged to callout details of the posts that will help with search-ability over time.

Over time, I will support this structure with better formatting across the site, which depends on my learning Hugo (the static site generator I’m using to produce this site) - which is a good example of an objective. For now, I will post objectives at the end of this page.

Finally, you can follow me on Twitter @NoSlackDev.
